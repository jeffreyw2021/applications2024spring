Information

  • The physical Product
    • Materials
      • Aluminum: Lightweight, strong
      • Steel: Durable, smooth ride
      • Carbon Fiber: High stiffness, lightweight, expensive
      • Titanium: Strength, lightweight, corrosion-resistant
    • Options
      • Gear Systems: Single-speed, multi-speed
      • Brake Types: Rim brakes, disc brakes
      • Suspension Systems: Rigid, front suspension, full suspension
    • Specifications
      • Frame Size: Various sizes, typically measured in cm or inches
      • Wheel Size: Commonly 26", 27.5", 29"
      • Gear Ratios: Depends on bike model and type
      • Weight: Varies from lightweight (~15 lbs) to heavier models (>30 lbs)
  • Product Variations
    • Sizes
      • Frame sizes typically range from XXS to XXL or 47cm to 64cm
      • Wheel sizes commonly include 20" (BMX), 24" (junior bikes), 26", 27.5", 29" (mountain bikes), and 700c (road bikes)
    • Types and Purposes
      • Road Bikes: For speed and pavement
      • Mountain Bikes: For off-road trails
      • Hybrid Bikes: For general-purpose riding
      • Electric Bikes: For assisted riding
      • BMX and Trick Bikes: For stunts and tricks
      • Foldable Bikes: For compact storage and travel
    • Quality, Performance, and Price Range
      • Entry-Level: $200 - $500, suitable for casual riders
      • Mid-Range: $500 - $1,500, offering better performance and durability
      • High-End: $1,500 - $5,000+, designed for serious or professional cyclists
      • Ultra-High-End: $5,000+, custom builds and top-tier components
    • Brands
      • Trek, Giant: Known for a wide range of quality bikes
      • Cannondale, BMC: Popular for high-performance road and mountain bikes
      • Scott, Yeti: Renowned in mountain biking circles
      • Pinarello, Bianchi: Famous for premium road bikes
      • Schwinn, Mongoose: Well-known for affordable bikes
      • Brompton, Dahon: Popular for foldable bikes
  • Bike Users
    • Demographics
      • Ages: All ages, from young children to seniors
      • Genders: All genders
      • Backgrounds: Diverse, from recreational riders to professional cyclists
    • Purpose
      • Commuting: Daily travel to work or school
      • Recreational: Leisure rides, family outings
      • Fitness: Exercise, training, endurance
      • Competitive: Racing, time trials, mountain biking
      • Off-road Adventure: Mountain biking, trail riding
    • Frequency of Use
      • Occasional: Weekend rides, sporadic use
      • Regular: Several times a week for commuting or exercise
      • Frequent: Daily or competitive training
    • Motivators
      • Health and Fitness: Improving physical well-being
      • Environmental Concerns: Eco-friendly transportation
      • Cost-Effectiveness: Affordable travel option
      • Passion for Cycling: Love of the sport and activity
      • Social Interaction: Group rides, community events
  • The Buying Experience
    • Where Bikes are Sold
      • Local Bike Shops: Personalized service, expert advice, fitting services
      • Big Box Retailers: More generalized selection, often less expensive
      • Online Stores: Wide selection, competitive pricing, reviews
    • By Whom
      • Trained Experts: In specialty bike shops for expert advice
      • General Retail Staff: In big box stores with broader product range
      • Online Platforms
    • Purchase Considerations
      • Type of Bike: Based on the intended use (road, mountain, hybrid, etc.)
      • Size and Fit: Crucial for comfort and efficiency, gender difference may also be considered
      • Quality and Price
      • Brand Reputation
      • After-sales Service: Access to maintenance, parts, and warranties
      • Accessories and Upgrades: Availability of additional components and customization options

How I picked and used my sources:

I'm a bike rider myself, so I had the experience of how to pick a bike and what to look for, and that is where majority of my knowledge came from. For more professional and technical aspect of information like bike wheel size, frame size, etc, I looked up on Wikipedia for specific parameters and numeric data. Then I also looked up two sources of articles that are more general and easy to understand for beginners so I can take on the perspective of someone who never had the experience of buying a bike before and see what information they will be exposed with before they begin their purchase.

Summary

  1. What big questions will a buyer will be faced with?

    Buyers will likely face decisions regarding the type of bike best suited for their activities, the size and fit for comfort and performance, and how much to invest considering quality and price. Brand reputation and what after-sales services are offered are also a key concern especially for new buyers. Additionally, buyers often consider the availability of accessories and the potential for future upgrades.

  2. What priorities might they have?

    Buyers' priorities may include the primary use of the bike (commuting, racing, leisure), comfort and ergonomic fit, durability and maintenance needs, budget constraints, and personal preferences in style or brand. Environmental impact and the desire for eco-friendly transportation options might also be a priority for some buyers, but may be less of a concern for others.

  3. What did you run into that you didn't understand, and now you do?

    Initially, I was more confused about the technical aspects of bike, such as the frame size, wheel sizes, and frame materials. Through this research, I now have a better understanding of how these factors influence the bike's performance, comfort, and suitability for different types of riding, as well as how they affect the bike's price. Also, it was very helpful for me to gather the technical and numerical data for these kind of information, as they will be very useful when it comes to constructing the information architecture of the website.
